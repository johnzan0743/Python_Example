Peakhurst brothel shut down - Local News - News - General - St George & Sutherland Shire Leader

Peakhurst brothel shut down

BY MARIA GALINOVIC

28 Sep, 2011 12:00 AM


 THE red light at the illegal brothel at Norman Street, Peakhurst, has been switched off along with water and electricity.
After almost two years of a cat and mouse game with the brothel, Hurstville Council took the matter to the Land and Environment Court.

The court issued an utilities order on August 24 which came into effect on September 9.

A nearby brothel owner, Sam, has been trying to get the council to close down the illegal competition for almost two years and said the legal businesses had been badly affected.

"They were a big problem  they used cheap labour, they lowered their prices and they tried to make as much money as possible before they were shut down," Sam said. "But this utilities order will shut them down because they can't operate without essential services."

Although in an industrial area, the brothel couldn't get a permit because a sex business was against the strata regulations and the other business owners in the complex objected.

The council issued about $37,000 in fines  none were paid  but said the operators proved impossible to pin down.

Each time the council issued a closure order, another development application would be submitted under a different name and the process would start again.

A council spokeswoman said it was pleased with the outcome.
