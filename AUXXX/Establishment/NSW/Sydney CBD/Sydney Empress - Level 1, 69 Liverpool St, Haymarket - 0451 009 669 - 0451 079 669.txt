That is a common misconception.

The way the law in NSW currently stands:

There is no such thing as a brothel (or FS) licence - or at least not in the same way as they have in Victoria or QLD with annual fees and licence numbers. All that is required is a Development Approval for a brothel to operate in the building (I suppose this could be viewed as a "de facto" licence).

BUT the definition of prostitution in the Legislation (the Summary Offences Act 1988) explicitly includes "masturbation committed by one person on another, for payment." So a "happy ending" massage is (according to the letter of the law) prostitution. And the Restricted Premises act defines a place where prostitution happens as a brothel (hardly surprising ...).
